<section id="tips">
<h2>7 Tips to Quit Smoking</h2>
<ul>
<li>Choose a quit date.</li>
<li>Remove cigarettes and lighters.</li>
<li>Stay active with exercise.</li>
<li>Drink plenty of water.</li>
<li>Avoid smoking triggers.</li>
<li>Ask family and friends for support.</li>
<li>Speak with a healthcare professional if you need extra help.</li>
</ul>
</section>

<section>
<h2>Benefits Timeline</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>20 Minutes:</strong> Heart rate begins returning to normal.</li>
<li><strong>24 Hours:</strong> Carbon monoxide levels drop.</li>
<li><strong>2–12 Weeks:</strong> Lung function and circulation improve.</li>
<li><strong>1 Year:</strong> Risk of heart disease is significantly lower than if you continued smoking.</li>
</ul>
</section>
